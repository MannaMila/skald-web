Questions before you set sail

A few clear answers.

Is the whole Odyssey included?

Yes. Skald includes all 24 books, not a sampler or a selection of famous episodes.

What do the 1-, 5-, and 20-minute labels mean?

They mark three levels of telling depth: a quick return to the shape of a book, a fuller sequence of scenes, and a sustained source-grounded telling. They are not exact reading-time guarantees.

Which translations are included?

Nine historical public-domain translations: six in English and one each in Spanish, French, and German. Skald does not include modern licensed translations.

Can I read the original Greek?

Yes. You can place the Greek beside a telling or translation, follow corresponding passages, and open the on-device Greek glossary.

Does Skald work offline?

The bundled reading library and reading tools work offline. External catalog and reference pages, online sharing services, analytics, and diagnostics need a connection.

Is this a subscription?

No. Skald is planned as a one-time purchase with no subscription, advertising, app account, or in-app purchases.

When and where will Skald be available?

Android, iPhone, and iPad are currently under store review. Initial availability is planned for the United States, Canada, Australia, and New Zealand.

What will the waitlist email me?

Only occasional Skald launch, platform-availability, and major product news. You can unsubscribe whenever you like.

Who publishes Skald?

MannaMila LLC publishes Skald independently. It is not affiliated with a film studio, publisher, museum, or modern translator.
